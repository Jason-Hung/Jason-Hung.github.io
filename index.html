Jason Hung

Software Developer Intern at 7D Surgical

Introduction

From May to December of 2019, I was working at 7D Surgical Inc as part of my first and second co-op work terms. There, I joined the software team as a Software Developer Intern. In this report, I will be detailing my responsibilities, my learning goals, and what I've accomplished during my 8 months there.

7D Surgical Inc.

7D Surgical is a medical device company specializing in image-guided surgery for spine and cranial navigation. Their system provides fast registration on the patient's anatomy, without the use of radiation. Traditional image-guided surgery systems may take up to 30 minutes to perform registration with the patient's anatomy, whereas the 7D Surgical System takes less than 20 seconds.

My day-to-day

As a Software Developer, I was responsible for helping develop and maintain software that is deployed onto the system. I helped design and implement UI and create new core features for user-facing applications. I was also responsible for documenting software design specifications and requirements, as required by various regulatory bodies such as Health Canada and the FDA.

I attended weekly meetings with the rest of the team to provide status updates and discuss scheduling of various projects. I also participated in code reviews to ensure quality of our software.

Workterm Focus

The focus for the first workterm was to gain an idea of what software development is like in the industry. Since I've never really had the chance to create software outside of school projects, I wanted to learn about standards and practices used in professional software development. I was also interested in seeing what the medical device industry is like with respect to software development, so I was also focused on learning how they operate.

The focus of the second workterm was to continue to develop my skills in design and applying best practices when developing software. During the first workterm, I realised that I enjoy designing, and the critical thinking that comes with it. I decided that I wanted to focus on getting better at it by helping to design more UI and new features for the projects I'm assigned to.

Learning Goals

As part of the University of Guelph's co-op program, I created 6 learning goals throughout the course of these two workterms.

Understand the processes and practices that the company uses to develop their software

Before I joined 7D Surgical, I've only worked on small school projects. Due to the scale of the projects, following standards and processes wasn't as necessary to ensure completion. Because of this, I felt that I didn't really know how software development worked in the industry. So one of the goals for this workterm was to improve myself as a developer by learning about the processes and practices that 7D Surgical follows when developing software so that I could apply them to my future work.

Understand the development tools and technologies available to me

Learn C#

Learn more about software architecture

Improve my UI/UX design skills

Since I started to get more tasks assigned to me that involved designing and refining UI, I found that I enjoyed the critical thinking that was required. As I continued working on these skills, I found that I was beginning to identify elements of the UI that might need improvement without having to ask my mentor for feedback on my prototypes.

Improve my skills in applying best practices to write consistent, clear, and maintainable software

For the first two years of my degree, I felt that the focus of the teachings were directed towards actually learning how to code, with only a small focus on the quality and maintainability of the code. I wasn't confident in the quality of the code for my school projects. At 7D Surgial, I was able to get a sense of when my code can be improved thanks to feedback I got from my pull requests. My mentor had complimented me on my ability to incorporate past feedback into my future work. I feel that I've now become more sensitive about having clean and maintainable code than I was when I first started.
